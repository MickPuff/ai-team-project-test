# Stakeholder Interview Guide (Kelly's Part - Gemini 1)

## Objective

Extract high-signal data for the residential back office automation MVP. Focus on value, pain, process, and source data.

---

## Owner & Executive

1. **North Star Metric:** "If you could only see one number every morning to know the business is healthy, what is it?"
2. **Decision Support:** "What is a recent decision where the data was missing, late, or unreliable?"
3. **Success Definition:** "In 6 months, what would make this system successful?"
4. **Automation Priority:** "Which manual back office task costs the most time or creates the most errors?"

## Finance & Admin

1. **The Utility Billing Loop:** "How do you get the meter readings for electricity and water? Is someone walking to each meter? How often are there mistakes?"
2. **Payment Tracking:** "How do you know if a tenant has paid their bill? Does the bank send you an alert, or do you check manually?"
3. **Reconciliation:** "Where do the numbers disagree most often?"
4. **Month-End Close:** "How many days after month-end does it take to finalize reports, and what causes delay?"

## Managers And Operations

1. **Occupancy Verification:** "How do you verify a room is actually vacant? Do you trust the paper log or do you have to physically check?"
2. **Maintenance Reporting:** "How do you currently report a broken pipe or room issue? Who is notified?"
3. **Room Status:** "Walk me through what happens when a room becomes vacant, occupied, under repair, or ready to rent."
4. **Shadow Systems:** "Do you keep any personal notes, notebooks, chat messages, or spreadsheets because the official source is hard to use?"

---

## Fieldwork Tips

- **Use the show-me rule:** Ask users to show the exact spreadsheet, report, form, or chat where the work happens.
- **Capture examples:** With permission, take photos or screenshots of whiteboards, paper logs, spreadsheet screens, and reports.
- **Separate fact from interpretation:** Record what users say first, then add analysis separately.
